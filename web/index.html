          Poker Glossary & Help
          
        

        

          
          
          
          
          
          
          
        

      

      


        

          
Table positions

          
Seats are named by where they sit relative to the dealer button. Acting later (more information) is an advantage.

          
BTNButton — the dealer seat. Acts last after the flop: the best position.

          
SBSmall Blind — posts a forced half-bet, sits left of the button, and acts first after the flop.

          
BBBig Blind — posts the full forced bet and acts last before the flop (gets the "option").

          
UTGUnder the Gun — first to act before the flop; the earliest, toughest spot.

          
MPMiddle Position — the seats between the early and late positions.

          
HJHijack — two seats right of the button; a late position.

          
COCutoff — one seat right of the button; a strong late position.

          
BTN/SBHeads-up (2 players): the button also posts the small blind and acts first before the flop.

          
ALL-INThe player has put all of their chips into the pot.

        


        

          
Betting rounds (streets)

          
A hand plays out over four rounds as shared "community" cards are revealed.

          
PreflopYou have your 2 hidden hole cards; no community cards yet. First round of betting.

          
FlopThe first 3 community cards are dealt face-up.

          
TurnA 4th community card.

          
RiverThe 5th and final community card.

          
ShowdownRemaining players reveal their cards; the best 5-card hand wins the pot.

        


        

          
Actions

          
FoldGive up the hand and any chips already in the pot.

          
CheckPass the action without betting — only possible when you owe nothing.

          
CallMatch the current bet to stay in the hand.

          
BetPut chips in when no one has bet yet this round.

          
RaiseIncrease the current bet, forcing others to call more or fold.

          
All-inBet every chip you have left.

        


        

          
Money & the pot

          
Blinds (SB / BB)Forced bets that seed the pot each hand, so there's always something to play for.

          
AnteAn extra small forced bet from everyone, when enabled.

          
PotAll the chips wagered so far — what you're playing to win.

          
Main / Side potWhen a player is all-in for less, later bets go into a side pot they can't win.

          
Pot oddsThe price to call, shown as ratio and % (e.g. 2.5:1 = 29%). Calling is profitable when your chance to win beats that %.

          
Min raiseThe smallest legal raise you're allowed to make.

          
StackThe chips you have left in front of you.

        


        

          
Bot playing styles

          
Each bot's label describes how it plays. Tight/loose = how many hands it plays; passive/aggressive = how often it bets & raises vs. just calls.

          
tight_passivePlays few hands and rarely raises — a cautious "rock".

          
tight_aggressiveFew hands, but bets and raises hard with them (TAG) — a strong, tough style.

          
loose_passivePlays lots of hands and mostly calls — a "calling station".

          
loose_aggressivePlays lots of hands and applies constant pressure (LAG).

          
maniacExtremely loose and aggressive; bluffs and raises relentlessly.

          
nitUltra-tight; only plays premium hands.

          
balancedMixes up its play — hard to read.

          
trapperPlays strong hands slowly to lure you into betting (slow-play).

        


        

          
Hand rankings (strongest first)

          
Straight FlushFive cards in sequence, all the same suit.

          
Four of a KindFour cards of the same rank.

          
Full HouseThree of a kind plus a pair.

          
FlushFive cards of the same suit (not in sequence).

          
StraightFive cards in sequence (mixed suits).

          
Three of a KindThree cards of the same rank.

          
Two PairTwo different pairs.

          
One PairTwo cards of the same rank.

          
High CardNone of the above — the highest card plays.

          
Short Deck (6+): a Flush beats a Full House, and A-6-7-8-9 counts as a straight.

        


        

          
Game setup

          
TournamentBlinds rise over time; bust and you're out (you'll be offered a rebuy here).

          
CashBlinds stay fixed; chips act like money and you can top up any time.

          
Blind levelHow many hands play before the blinds increase (tournament only).

          
Short deckPlays with a 36-card deck (2–5 removed): more big hands and adjusted rankings.
